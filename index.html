May I

A Goulet and Borden Family specialty

Deck Setup

For 4 people, use 2 decks. For 5 or 6 people, use 3 decks

The Deal

The dealer deals 11 cards to each player. The player to the left of the dealer is dealt 12 cards. This player starts the game by choosing a card to discard.

Target Hands

  • #6 - 2 sets of 3
  • #7 - 1 set of 3, 1 run of 4
  • #8 - 2 runs of 4
  • #9 - 3 sets of 3
  • #10 - 1 set of 3, 1 run of 7
  • #11 - 2 sets of 3, 1 run of 5
  • #12 - 3 runs of 4
  • #13 - 1 set of 3, 1 run of 10
  • #14 - 3 sets of 3, 1 run of 5
  • #15 - 3 runs of 5, no discard allowed

In all cases you can have more cards in your sets or runs before laying down

3 May I's maximum per hand, or 17 cards total in your hand

After you lay your runs and sets down for each hand, you can then play any remaining cards in your hand on anyone else's cards that are down. Any wild card in a run can be replaced with the actual card, then move the wild card up or down in the run. When a run is full from Ace through 3, that run is dead and cannot be played on.

Aces are always high

2's and Jokers are wild cards

Scoring

  • 3-9 count 5 points each
  • 10-K count 10 points each
  • Aces count 20 points each
  • 2's count 20 points each
  • Jokers count 50 points each

After playing all hands the person with the lowest total is the winner
